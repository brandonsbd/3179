Australian Domestic Airlines On-Time Performance

The Bureau of Infranstructure, Transport and Regional Economics monitors the punctuality and reliability of major domestic airlines operating between Australian airports. The original dataset contains data from 2004, however to keep relevancy only data from 2020 onwards will be contributed to the visualization. The purpose of this visualization is to present the data in a easily interpretable way to assist the evaluation of industry performance and aid air travel consumers in making informed decisions.

Flight Performance Insights

In this section, overall performance of airlines in Australia is analysed, focusing on key metrics such as on-time performance, cancellations, and overall reliability. This data is intended to provide insights into how airlines have been operating over the years and help identify trends or issues that may impact travelers.

This scatter plot visualizes the relationship between the number of sectors scheduled and the number of on-time departures for different airlines. Each point on the scatter plot represents data for a specific airline in terms of how many flights were scheduled and how many of those flights departed on time.

The chart visualizes the percentage of flights that were canceled out of the total number of scheduled flights for each airline.

Qantas and Virgin Australia appear to be strong, reliable airlines with fewer performance issues, while Tigerair Australia and Bonza exhibit more variability in their punctuality. The gradual recovery in on-time performance across most airlines in 2023 and 2024 may indicate that the industry is stabilizing after the disruptions caused by the pandemic.
